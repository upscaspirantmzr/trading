Detailed Trade Entry Checklist

Ensure you've considered these points before executing a trade:

  • Market Trend Confirmed?

    Is the overall market (Nifty/BankNifty) trend aligned with your trade direction? Avoid counter-trend trades unless part of a specific strategy.

  • Strategy Rules Met?

    Have all entry conditions for your chosen strategy been satisfied? (e.g., indicator crosses, price action patterns, volume confirmation).

  • Risk Defined?

    Is your Stop Loss (SL) clearly identified and acceptable for your capital? Never enter a trade without a predefined exit for loss.

  • Reward Potential Clear?

    Do you have a target (TP) and is the Risk-Reward Ratio favorable (e.g., 1:2 or better)?

  • Position Sizing Correct?

    Is the quantity (lots) appropriate for your risk management plan, risking only a small percentage of your capital per trade?

  • No Major News Events Due?

    Check for upcoming economic data, corporate earnings, or other news that could cause high volatility.

A quick reminder of my core principles:

  • "Trade with the trend, not against it."
  • "Your Stop Loss is your best friend. Respect it."
  • "Never risk more than 1-2% of capital on a single trade."

Common Trading Issues & Solutions

Overtrading / Revenge Trading

Issue: Taking too many trades, especially after a loss, leading to further losses.

Solution: Implement a strict daily trade limit. After hitting your loss limit, close the terminal. Review your journal to understand triggers.

Not Sticking to Stop Loss (SL)

Issue: Moving your SL or not exiting when your trade hits your predefined risk level.

Solution: Place physical SL orders. Trust your analysis; if SL is hit, the trade idea was wrong. Focus on capital preservation.

Exiting Winners Too Early

Issue: Booking profits prematurely, missing out on larger moves.

Solution: Implement trailing stop losses. Let winners run. Review trades where you exited early to see missed profit potential.

Lack of Post-Trade Analysis

Issue: Not learning from past trades, repeating the same mistakes.

Solution: Consistently use this journal! Document your setup, entry, exit, and most importantly, your learnings from every trade.
